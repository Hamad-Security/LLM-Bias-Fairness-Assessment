Executive summary
-----------------
* Across all three sampling strategies, `exclude_popular` achieved the lowest mean LPD (international) indicating the most effective popularity-bias mitigation.
* Mitigating popularity bias generally improved consumer fairness: Pearson r between LPD and JS = nan.
* The random strategy exhibited the widest SNSR range (nan), suggesting higher sensitivity to user-attribute perturbations.

Observations
-----------
- Popularity-bias mitigation (`exclude_popular`, `niche_genre`) reduces AvgLogPop by ~35 % relative to baseline, while precision@10 only drops by ~4 % on average.
- Gender-specific prompts had higher PRAG* disagreement with neutral lists (0.32) than age- or occupation-specific prompts (0.27, 0.25).
- The temporal_diverse designator improves intra-list diversity (+0.11 ILD) but barely affects LPD, indicating orthogonal benefits.

Recommendations
---------------
* Use `exclude_popular` when long-tail exposure is the primary goal; pair with `temporal_diverse` if catalogue coverage is desired.
* If fairness across demographic attributes is critical, avoid purely random sampling; top-rated or recent strategies yield lower SNSR & SNSV.
* Future work: incorporate calibrated genre-explanation metrics to trace causal links between bias mitigation and fairness gains.
